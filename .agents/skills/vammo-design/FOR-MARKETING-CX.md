# Vammo brand, in plain language

This is the no-jargon version of the Vammo Design System — for Marketing, CX, People, or
anyone putting together a deck, post, poster, or message that needs to look and sound like
Vammo, without needing to know what a "token" or "shadcn" is. For code-level detail (the kind
an engineer needs), see `README.md` in this same folder instead.

## What Vammo stands for

Vammo empowers bikers to control how they move — toward a better present and a future worth
having. The line that captures it: **"o corre não para"** (the hustle never stops). Three words
describe the brand's personality: **Visionary, Connector, Explorer.** When you're not sure if
something "sounds like Vammo," ask: would a Visionary-Connector-Explorer say or show this?

## Colors — what to actually use

Vammo's colors are **black, white, and one bright blue** (`#2EC2FF`, sometimes called "Neon
Blue"). That blue is the color you reach for by default — buttons, links, highlights, the one
splash of color on a slide.

There are three more bright colors — yellow, pink, orange — but they're **not decoration**.
Use them only when the color itself means something: yellow for "passed/available," pink for
"failed," orange for "warning/in progress." If a slide or post feels like it needs "more color,"
the answer is more blue, or black-and-white contrast — not reaching for yellow/pink/orange.

If you're comparing three things (three suppliers, three plans, three regions), don't give each
one a different neon color. Give the lead/main one blue, and tell the rest apart with gray
tones or layout instead.

## Type & voice

- On decks and marketing: lowercase headlines feel right — "o corre não para," not "O Corre Não
  Para." Product screens (the app, BackOffice) use normal sentence case.
- Portuguese (BR), informal "você" — never "tu," never "o cliente."
- Short, direct, a little playful. No corporate hedging ("pode ser que," "estamos avaliando") —
  say what is.
- No emoji in the product. Marketing can use arrows (→, ↓) if it helps.
- Example — good: *"bateria zerada? troca em 60s."* Avoid: *"Tenha a melhor experiência de
  troca!"* — that's the kind of overselling tone we don't use.

## The logo

- Files: black, white, or blue wordmark. **Black** is the default (use on white, on photos, on
  the blue accent). **White** only goes on a black background. **Blue** is for moments where you
  want the accent to carry into the logo itself.
- Leave breathing room around it — don't crowd the logo with other elements.
- Don't stretch it, rotate it more than 45°, recolor it, put it in a box, or add a drop shadow.

## The "Zimmzag" — Vammo's signature graphic

That wavy "mmm" pattern that shows up in Vammo materials has an official name: **Zimmzag** (not
"zigzag" — the file names on disk say zigzag, but call it Zimmzag in conversation and in
copy). It comes in blue, black, and white versions. Rule of thumb: it should always bleed off
the edge of whatever it's on — never leave a margin around it, and never let it end awkwardly
in the middle of a layout.

## Photography

Real, warm, natural-light photos of São Paulo life — riders, the city, community, the bike and
battery up close. Not staged studio shots, not "night-neon" stock photography.

## One character to retire

There used to be a cartoon mascot ("Vamminho" / "Little Guy") in Vammo materials. **It's
retired as of 2026** — don't use it in anything new. If you need an illustration-style element,
use the Zimmzag pattern or a real photo instead.

## Quick checklist before sending something out

- Blue is the accent; other bright colors only if they mean something specific.
- Logo is black (default), unedited, with room to breathe.
- Copy is direct, informal "você," no emoji, no corporate hedging.
- If there's a wavy graphic pattern involved, it bleeds off the edge — call it Zimmzag.
- No cartoon mascot.

If something doesn't fit into any of the above and you're not sure, ask an engineer or check
`README.md` — it has the full, detailed version with exact colors and measurements.
